The rain didn’t just fall in Oakhaven; it drifted like unspooled gray silk, tangling in the iron streetlamps and the whiskers of the stray cats. In the center of the town square stood the "Clock of Minor Inconveniences," a brass monstrosity that didn’t track hours, but rather the exact moment someone within a five-mile radius would lose a single sock or find their tea had gone lukewarm.

Arthur, the town’s reluctant horologist, was currently elbow-deep in the clock’s gears, trying to fish out a rogue peppermint patty.

"It’s a structural hazard, Arthur," a voice croaked from below.

Arthur peered down. It was Barnaby, a crow who wore a miniature monocle and claimed to be the reincarnation of a very disappointed librarian.

"The peppermint?" Arthur asked, wiping grease on his apron.

"No, the existential dread," Barnaby replied, fluttering up to a nearby gargoyle. "The clock is ticking backward. That hasn't happened since the Great Hiccup of '84. If it hits zero, everyone in town is going to forget how to use doorknobs for at least twenty minutes."

Arthur sighed. He’d seen worse. Last Tuesday, the clock had malfunctioned and caused every umbrella in the city to turn into a bouquet of very confused celery.

He tightened a bolt, adjusted the main escapement, and watched as the brass hands shuddered. Suddenly, the gears began to hum a low, melodic tune—the sound of a thousand tiny harps. The "Minor Inconvenience" display shifted from Losing a Library Card to Pleasant Surprise.

Across the street, a woman named Martha found a five-dollar bill in a coat she hadn't worn in years. In the bakery, the sourdough rose perfectly without any effort at all.

Arthur climbed down, his knees popping like bubble wrap. "Fixed it," he muttered.

"For now," Barnaby warned, preening a wing. "But I’d keep an eye on the pendulum. It’s starting to look a bit too much like a giant slice of watermelon, and that never bodes well for the local gravity."

Arthur looked up, saw the pinkish hue reflecting off the brass, and decided that was a problem for tomorrow. Today, the tea was hot, the socks were matched, and the doorknobs were working perfectly.
